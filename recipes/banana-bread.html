Walnut Banana Bread

Return Home

Description

This recipe is for making banana bread with chopped walnuts. You can find the original recipe here. It's cheap and easy to make. Every week when I go grocery shopping, I buy a bunch of bananas to bring to work for lunch, and at the end of the week I make banana bread with the bananas that are left over. The recipe calls for two bananas but you can use three bananas, it might just be a little more moist.

You can also freeze the bananas to preserve them before making the bread, the peels will turn orange. The bananas will liquify making it difficult to peel them so I recommend peeling them before freezing them, and also slicing them first too. Be sure to properly defrost them before using them.

I personally like to melt the butter slowly in a sauce pan first, rather than use a microwave. Remember 1/2 a cup of butter is just a stick of butter. I do not "flour" the pan I just spray it down with olive oil. I personally like to use 1 cup of white all purpose flour and 1/2 of whole wheat flour. You can substitute chocolate chips for walnuts if you want chocolate chip banana bread.

Ingredients

  • 3/4 cup sugar
  • 1/2 cup butter, softened
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 medium (1 cup) bananas, mashed
  • 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup chopped walnuts
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground cinnamon

Steps

  1. Heat oven to 350Â°F. Grease and flour 8x4-inch loaf pan; set aside.
  2. Combine sugar, butter and eggs in bowl. Beat at medium speed, scraping bowl often, until creamy. Add banana and vanilla. Beat at low speed until well mixed. Stir in all remaining ingredients.
  3. Spoon batter into prepared pan. Bake 60-65 minutes or until toothpick inserted in center comes out clean. Cool in pan 10 minutes; remove from pan. Place onto cooling rack. Cool completely.
  4. Serve with butter or toppings as desired.
